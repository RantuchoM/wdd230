History

Foundation:Bahía Blanca was founded in 1828 by Colonel Ramón Estomba, under the orders of Juan Manuel de Rosas, as a fortress to protect the area from cattle rustlers and Brazilian invaders. It was named after the white salt deposits that covered the shores of the bay, which was discovered by Ferdinand Magellan in 1520.

Growth: The city grew rapidly after the arrival of the railway in 1885, which facilitated the export of grain and wool from the region. It also attracted many immigrants from Europe, especially from Spain, Italy, France, Germany, and Eastern Europe.

Industry and Commerce: Today, Bahía Blanca is an important industrial and commercial center, as well as a gateway to the Patagonia. It has an important seaport with a depth of 15 m, where the Napostá Stream drains.

Bahía Blanca Chamber of Commerce History

The Bahía Blanca Chamber of Commerce was founded in 1896 by a group of local businessmen who wanted to promote the economic development of the city and the region. They saw the potential of Bahía Blanca as a strategic port for the export of agricultural and industrial products, as well as a gateway to the Patagonia. They also wanted to attract more immigrants and investors from Europe and other countries, as well as to defend the interests of the local merchants and entrepreneurs.

The Chamber of Commerce played an important role in the modernization and expansion of the city, supporting the construction of public works, such as roads, bridges, railways, waterworks, and electricity. It also organized trade fairs, exhibitions, conferences, and cultural events to showcase the products and services of Bahía Blanca and to foster the exchange of ideas and knowledge. It also established relations with other chambers of commerce and business associations from Argentina and abroad, creating a network of cooperation and solidarity.

The Chamber of Commerce faced many challenges and difficulties throughout its history, such as wars, economic crises, social conflicts, and political changes. However, it always maintained its vision and mission of contributing to the progress and well-being of Bahía Blanca and its people. Today, the Chamber of Commerce continues to be a representative and influential institution in the city, with more than 2,000 members from different sectors and activities. It also keeps working to improve the competitiveness and sustainability of Bahía Blanca, as well as to strengthen its position as a regional hub and a global partner.
